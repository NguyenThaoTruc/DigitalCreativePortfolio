Project Overview

As Head of Design and Media in the Social Work Team at Le Hong Phong High School for the Gifted, I led the creative direction for our most impactful annual initiative—a two-part charity campaign consisting of the Neverland Workshop, a fundraising event, and The Little Heroes Project (also known as "Kế hoạch Siêu nhí"), which supported children at the SOS Village in Ho Chi Minh City.

I oversaw the full visual strategy, including branding, photography, videography, and storytelling, to ensure every touchpoint conveyed empathy and purpose. Thanks to the strength of our visual communication, the Workshop attracted over 300 in-person participants and more than 2,500 online supporters. This experience not only allowed me to lead a creative team but also showed me the power of design in driving real-world social impact.

PART 1: NEVERLAND WORKSHOP

(Click on each image to view it in full size.)

In the first phase of the event journey, we organized 2–3 DIY workshops. The ticket fees paid by participants were considered direct contributions to our fundraising goal.

The campaign included a series of three promotional posts, all of which I contributed to. For the teaser post that served as a visual hint and sparked curiosity (the first image on the left), I illustrated the artwork in Adobe Illustrator and animated it into the video shown below. I also actively contributed to the other illustrations (the 2 remainings), led the event's visual media, and oversaw the design of all props.

This animated video brings to life the core concept of our campaign—a whimsical toy factory in motion. It visualizes the magical world behind the scenes, where creativity and kindness come together to "produce" joy for the children we support.

PART 2: THE LITTLE HEROES PROJECT

(Click on each image to view it in full size.)

In the second phase of the project, all proceeds from the workshops were used to support a charity visit to the SOS Children’s Village in Ho Chi Minh City.

Featured below are photographs from our official media campaign, which I led as Photography Director and Photo editor. I developed the visual concept, directed the technical team during the photoshoots, and personally edited each image to ensure a consistent and emotionally resonant narrative.

Below is the recap video of our charity visit. Together with three other members of the Design–Technical team, I contributed to capturing a wide range of beautiful footage. I also served as the sole video editor for the final film.

One highlight of this experience was collaborating with the Content team to successfully reach out to renowned artists Đen Vâu and PiaLinh for music rights approval—an effort that added both emotional depth and authenticity to the final piece.
