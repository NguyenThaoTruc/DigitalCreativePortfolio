THE GLORY

A Storytelling Illustration - School Psychological Project

Please scroll down to activate the project

(Click on each image to view it in full size.)

Studying at a prestigious high school, I have experienced significant pressure throughout my academic journey, and I recognize that my peers face similar challenges. To address this, I initiated a psychological project aimed at fostering empathy and raising awareness about the critical importance of work-life balance.

Drawing inspiration from the lily flower, which symbolizes confidence, strength, and beauty, I want to create the process of achieving success through the image of a flower-liked character striving to climb the stairs to reach the pinnacle of glory, and understand more about herself.

The journey begins.

With her first steps on the stairs, the protagonist encounters several flower buds, much like herself—carefree and immature as she faces the initial hardships. As she ascends, the flowers bloom more beautifully with each step. However, along with their floral elegance, she begins to notice the increasing presence of thorny vines, which instill fear, uncertainty, and hesitation whenever she prepares to move forward.

Determined enough, she overcomes her deep feelings of unease and finally reaches the top of the stairs, where she discovers the light of her inner soul.


In the grasp of the shining light, she realizes both her strength and, at the same time, her vulnerability. Throughout her journey, challenges and difficulties have severely "injured" her without her being aware, as she was solely focused on her goal.
Now, she understands that while success is important, it’s even more crucial to learn to

“Love yourself, know your abilities, and rest whenever you feel the need.”

This is the message I want to convey through the story.
